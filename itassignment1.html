Turbulent Logician

After using 16 personalities, it had decided that I am a Turbulent Logician.

Strengths

This test and the other tests that I have taken have given me a little insight to who I am as a person, but it hasn't been anything mind blowing. I feel as if I have discovered an in-depth description of words that I would describe myself as. Although this is this case, I have also found some useful information that I will refernce in the form of dot points and then go into how I feel about what "16Personalities" has described.

  • Great Analysts and Abstract Thinkers – People with the Logician personality type view the world as a big, complex machine, and recognize that as with any machine, all parts are interrelated. Logicians excel in analyzing these connections, seeing how seemingly unrelated factors tie in with each other in ways that bewilder most other personality types.
  • Open-Minded – Logicians couldn’t make these connections if they thought they knew it all – they are highly receptive to alternate theories, so long as they’re supported by logic and facts. In more subjective matters like social norms and traditions, Logicians are usually fairly liberal, with a “none of my business” sort of attitude – peoples’ ideas are what matter.

    • These strengths are the ones that I have chosen from 16Personalities to describe me best. I believe I have the ability to keep an open mind about anything I do. Especially with people and working in groups. Allowing everybody to speak their opinions and ideas is a big part of having a team function without many hiccups.

    Weaknesses

  • Insensitive – Oftentimes Logician personalities get so caught up in their logic that they forget any kind of emotional consideration – they dismiss subjectivity as irrational and tradition as an attempt to bar much-needed progress. Purely emotional situations are often utterly puzzling to Logicians, and their lack of timely sympathy can easily offend.
  • Very Private and Withdrawn – While Logicians’ intellectualism yields many insights into their surroundings, their surroundings are ironically considered an intrusion on their thoughts. This is especially true with people – Logicians are quite shy in social settings. More complicated situations such as parties exacerbate this, but even close friends struggle to get into Logicians’ hearts and minds.

    • These weaknesses described are fairly accurate in my opinon. Whereas I do trust myself being open minded, I am someone who will spill out something I shouldn't say or will hurt someones feelings. But I also believe honesty is the best policy. As of being private and withdrawn, I have even noticed this in my online RMIT work. I don't contribute to discussion forums as often as I should and I do have overwhelming anxiety a lot of the time. Tending to keep to myself isn't something that should be implemented into a group project but I am also working on these weaknesses with forms of psychology.
